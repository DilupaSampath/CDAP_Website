Along with the growth of knowledge and technology large number of methods and new modules were developed not only to identify the disease, also for prediction of Dengue outbreak. A productive prediction module should be able to detect the unusual occurrences of Dengue fever and it will help to control the rise of the endemic in specified area. One of the applications that use time series forecasting techniques is the system to model emergency medical system calls in Calgary, Alberta. The objective of this research was to offer simple and effective models that could be used for forecasting daily and hourly call volumes. For this they have used three main approaches, standard regression ignoring dependencies, regression models with correlated residuals, and a third approach doubly-seasonal ARIMA. Several researches are made to address the same problem in Sri Lanka using Artificial Neural Networks (ANN). But none of them seems to have sufficient accuracy towards testing data.

Early Warning System was developed to predict Dengue outbreaks in Sri Lanka using Neural Networks. It used regression to identify risk factors and ANN for predictions. Training accuracy for ANN model has been high for Matara (83.6%) and Colombo (89.5%). But there was a rapid decrease in testing accuracy for both Matara (62%) and Colombo (67%). Ana Carvalho & Ricardo Pereira of University of Porto has done a research on management and scheduling of hospital resources.

The research work on focused on optimizing healthcare staff in the Pediatric Department of Prince Sultan Military Medical City (PSMMC) using GA with cost bit matrix. The main goal of their optimization was to satisfy doctors scheduling problem as much as possible while fulfilling the employers’ requirements. Their results showed that the suggested method is very useful and able to give reasonable solutions to the problem fast compared to the traditional manual methods.

Iterated Local Search is employed to get an improvement for the place resource schedule problem. Here a mathematical model is given and develop a hybrid heuristic method on the basis of resource constrained project scheduling problem and packing problem, where it is able to get an initial solution for the iterated local search by using this method. Finally, feasibility is verified with the use of simulate data.

The existing hospital management system in IDH only has currently doesn’t contain a proper management system for its dengue patients and doctors. Not only the IDH Hospital, all the hospitals in Sri Lanka use the traditional data entering and updating systems to manage information about patients and doctors. Another problem that we observed is that the working schedules for nurses are maintained manually which leads to many difficulties since when creating schedules for nurses, factors such as the patient count and the current situation of the wards are hardly considered. The proposed system should be able to automate that task. When creating the schedules, factors such as; number of nurses , number of patients, working hours, number of wards are considered
Research Problem Nonexistent of a proper systematic hospital management system. Patients are not allocated to the doctors systematically. Patients aren’t allocated to the relevant wards based on their health condition (improved or worsen). Working schedules of the nurses are maintained manually. Nurses are able to apply for leave only by considering the current situation. Nurses being insufficient according to the number of patients
We aim to develop a better hospital management system for Dengue wards in IDH Hospital as per their requirement which would highly benefit the doctors and nurses and would increase their productivity in decision making. System will consist of two main components named, Optimizing component and Prediction Component where Prediction component will predict the future patient count and Optimizing Component will generate schedules and leave plans for office staff based on the output of Prediction Module. We’ve implemented each model using two algorithms. We’ve conducted several tests and experiments on each model to select the best algorithm which gives best results. Most productive model will be integrated to the system.

Prediction Model

We compared both prediction models considering accuracy and time taken to train both models. According to our test results we’ve came to the conclusion that RNN with LSTM gives better accuracy than ARIMA.

Optimizing model

We have conducted experiments to measure the performances of our optimization algorithms using the statistics collected from IDH. With the real data sets, our results show that, GA and ILS can decide the optimal allocations dynamically in the order of seconds. Also, the results show that the GA approach is much efficient and faster than the ILS approach.

Optimizing Module - Genatic

IDH hospital currently uses a non-optimal nurse allocation platform where nurses are allocated to different wards inefficiently and randomly and the schedules were generated in a slower rate. Research area includes to generate schedules efficiently and faster compared to the ILS approach using real data sets. Following are the goals achieved by resolving the issues faced by the hospital related to optimization



  • Generate optimal working schedules for nurses
  • Suggest leave plans for nurses according to the existing number of patients and the predicted patient count for a particular period of time.
  • Generate a report which provides the minimum number of nurses that should be available for a particular period of time.
